Do Less and Achieve More: Free Condition Video Outpainting with Diffusion Model

Paper

Abstract

Video outpainting aims to extend the content of avideo beyond its original spatial boundaries. Existing methods tends to condition the generation process on a single frame or caption, failing to address the challenge in long videos with multiple video clips. To address this, we extend the diffusion-based image inpainting to a 3D diffusion model with a motion module for video outpainting. Then, we design four conditioning schemes to seek the appropriate condition strategy: Frame-wise context, Frame-wise Adapter, Text and Free Condition. Additionally, to enhance the continuity in long video genera- tion, we propose a noval Motion Momentum Update (MMU) method based on a training-free technique. Experimental results demonstrate that our proposed Free Condition strategy, termed Free-Outpainter, achieves state-of-the-art performance in video outpainting tasks. Ablation study also shows that the condition- free training paradigm is the most effective, avoiding inaccurate external information and fully utilizing inherent video data. Benefiting from the Free Condition strategy, our pipeline reduces the inference time to 5 seconds for 512x512x16 frames with only 10 GB of GPU memory.

Pipeline

The overview of our proposed Video Outpainting model. During training, only the Temporal Attention layer is trained, while the other layers are frozen. We explore four different conditional control training strategies: Frame-wise context, Frame-wise Adapter, Text and Free Condition.
